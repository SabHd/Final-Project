Understanding the Threats

An interactive framework covering Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTDs) and epidemic infectious diseases. Use the filters below to explore critical pathogens, transmission routes, and current eradication strategies.

Parasitic Nematode

Onchocerciasis (River Blindness)

Caused by parasitic worms transmitted by blackflies, leading to severe itching, skin disease, and irreversible blindness if untreated.

Transmission: Blackfly bites (Simulium spp.)
Impact: Blindness, severe skin disease
RLMF / Field Role: Directed country-led elimination programs across Africa & Yemen
Parasitic Worm

Lymphatic Filariasis (Elephantiasis)

Spread by mosquitoes, this disease causes painful, extreme swelling of limbs and structural tissues, inducing severe social stigma and disability.

Transmission: Mosquito bites
Impact: Extreme elephantiasis, lifelong physical disability
RLMF / Field Role: Funding mass drug administration (MDA) campaigns to stop community transmission
Bacterial Infection

Trachoma (Bacterial Eye Infection)

A highly contagious bacterial infection spread via poor hygiene and common flies, causing inner eyelid scarring and complete blindness.

Transmission: Direct contact, flies, poor hygiene
Impact: Irreversible corneal blindness
RLMF / Field Role: Country-led SAFE strategy implementations (Surgery, Antibiotics, Face-washing, Environmental change)
Parasite Eradication

Guinea Worm Disease (Dracunculiasis)

A painful, debilitating parasitic infection contracted by drinking stagnant water contaminated with flea larvae, emerging slowly through the skin.

Transmission: Stagnant water intake
Impact: Excruciating skin emergence, temporary/permanent disability
Field Partnership: Carter Center active surveillance led in-field by Dr. Hamdi (Chad)
Epidemic Vector

Malaria (Plasmodium Parasite)

While not classified as an NTD, malaria is a massive global focus, resulting in widespread acute illness and pediatric deaths across tropical climates.

Transmission: Anopheles mosquito bites
Impact: Severe cyclical fevers, organ failure, child mortality
Programs: Roll Back Malaria Partnership, GLIDE, and regional RTS,S vaccination programs
Viral Pathogen

Polio (Poliomyelitis)

A highly infectious viral disease that attacks the nervous system, capable of inducing irreversible flaccid paralysis within hours.

Transmission: Fecal-oral route
Impact: Neurological paralysis, severe long-term disability
GPEI Integration: Massive global vaccination networks; targeted campaigns in Pakistan
